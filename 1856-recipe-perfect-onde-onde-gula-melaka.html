Let me just give you a brief introduction to this unique Malay dessert we call buah melaka. This bite-sized green glutinous rice ball is coated in grated coconut (yes, fresh grated coconut and not dessicated coconut please) with oozing gula melaka (palm sugar) syrup in the middle. Mix with granulated sugar and coconut oil until the filling binds together. You can have Onde-onde gula melaka using 8 ingredients and 5 steps. Here is how you achieve it.

Ingredients of Onde-onde gula melaka

  1. You need of Tepung pulut.
  2. You need of sudu Tepung gandum.
  3. It's of Daun pandan @ 4 titis perisa pandan.
  4. It's of Perwarna hijau.
  5. You need of Gula merah cube(potong dadu).
  6. You need of Air cuckoo.
  7. You need of Garam.
  8. It's of Hampas kelapa.

Divide into small balls, set aside. The filling solidifies when the onde onde is left to sit at room temperature (at least where I live). Just like the sweet potato onde onde that I made at here, they are chewy and also tender! The dough is also easier to handle and I can incorporate more gula Melaka filling than usual!

Onde-onde gula melaka step by step

  1. Blend daun pandan bersama 1 cawan suam air cuckoo menggunakan max blender cuckoo, tapis & ambil air nye-jike tiada daun pandan skip step ini- gantikan dengan 4 titis perisa pandan bersama 1 cawan air cuckoo.
  2. Masukkan tepung pulut,tepung gandum, 1 cawan air pandan, pewarna (3titis),garam-sy tak masukkan perwarna sbb pewarna tiada-gaulkan & uli.
  3. Ambil sedikit doh,bulat & leperkan masukkan gula merah ditengah,tutup & bulatkan semula..ulangi sampai habis doh.
  4. Reneh air cuckoo, masukkan doh-doh kecil ke dalam air mengelegak, biarkan sehingga timbul-jika sudah timbul doh-doh telah masak.
  5. Kemudian hampas kelapa digaul bersama sedikit garam-masukkan doh-doh yg dimasak & gaul & hidang.

Onde-onde is one of the traditional kuih in Malaysia; they are one of my favorites. Kuih is term for Malaysian sweet cakes or pastries. These cute little onde-onde-also spelled as ondeh-obdeh-are infused with pandan (screwpine leaf) juice and filled with "Gula Melaka" or palm sugar. In Malaysia and Singapore, onde-onde is referred to these delicious glutinous rice balls filled with gula melaka (palm sugar).
